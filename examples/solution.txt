Since $a+b+c=0$, we have
\[
(a+b+c)^2 = a^2+b^2+c^2+2ab+2bc+2ca = 0.
\]
Rearranging gives
\[
a^2+b^2+c^2 = -2(ab+bc+ca).
\]
So
\[
a^2+b^2+c^2-(ab+bc+ca) = -3(ab+bc+ca).
\]
An equivalent standard form is
\[
(a-b)^2+(b-c)^2+(c-a)^2 = 2(a^2+b^2+c^2-ab-bc-ca) \ge 0,
\]
which proves
\[
a^2+b^2+c^2 \ge ab+bc+ca.
\]
